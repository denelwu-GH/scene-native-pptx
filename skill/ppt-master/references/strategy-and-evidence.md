# Strategy, Storyline, And Evidence

## Source Audit

Before writing, inventory every source deck, document, image, and approved statement. Record the source-of-truth order. Prefer source PPT text or structured documents over OCR.

Separate three boundaries:

- immutable: user-protected text, layout, page selection, logo, or legal wording;
- editable: copy, hierarchy, examples, and visual treatment the user permits changing;
- excluded: pages and materials that must not enter the output.

## Storyline Rules

Every slide needs:

- one audience question;
- one communication role;
- one sentence the audience should remember;
- one bridge from the previous slide and one transition to the next;
- one explicit evidence class.

Recommended roles include context, tension, insight, proof, mechanism, scenario, decision, roadmap, and appendix. Avoid repeating the same role across several consecutive slides unless the deck is an evidence catalogue.

## Claim Maturity

Use only these maturity values:

- `public-verified`: supported by a public authoritative source;
- `internal-verified`: supported by an approved internal source;
- `poc`: demonstrated in a controlled proof of concept;
- `target-experience`: an intended product or service experience;
- `vision`: a directional future state.

For every claim, record the permitted wording and evidence status. A claim cannot be `public-verified` or `internal-verified` while its evidence is open. Target and vision claims must be visibly labeled and may not use deployed-capability language.

## Page Contract

Create one contract per slide with:

- source page and target slide ID;
- purpose and takeaway;
- story role and evidence class;
- exact-text or rewrite policy;
- required elements and forbidden changes;
- claim IDs;
- visual pattern and first focal point;
- accessibility title, reading order, and alt-text requirements;
- acceptance criteria.

Do not use the reference image as semantic authority. The page contract and approved source content remain authoritative.

## Approval

Strategy approval covers storyline, page roles, exact text, claims, and maturity labels. Design approval covers visual references, hierarchy, brand expression, and composition. Keep these approvals separate so a visual change cannot silently rewrite an approved claim.
